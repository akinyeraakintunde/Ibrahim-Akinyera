Enterprise Risk Intelligence Engine

Evidence document – Tech Nation Global Talent (Digital Technology)
Prepared by: Ibrahim Akinyera

1. Executive Summary

The Enterprise Risk Intelligence Engine is an AI-enabled cybersecurity and technology risk analytics system that ingests enterprise system logs, detects suspicious events, scores them using Key Risk Indicators (KRIs), and generates structured risk intelligence reports. The project demonstrates end-to-end capability in architecture design, Python engineering, risk modelling and collaboration with an enterprise Technology Risk Analyst at CME Group.

2. Problem and Purpose

Modern organisations generate large volumes of logs from authentication systems, applications, devices and networks. Technology Risk and Security teams must identify high-risk behaviour, prioritise incidents and communicate risk clearly, but manual analysis does not scale.

The purpose of this project is to build a reusable engine that:

  • Ingests and parses enterprise-style logs.
  • Identifies anomalies aligned to real Technology Risk KRIs.
  • Assigns risk scores and levels (Low/Medium/High/Critical).
  • Produces audit-ready HTML risk intelligence reports.

3. Technical Architecture

Layers:

  • Log Ingestion Layer: Loads CSV logs (e.g. logins, failures, privilege events, data exports).
  • Anomaly Detection Layer: Applies rules and KRIs to flag suspicious events.
  • Risk Scoring Engine: Converts anomalies into numeric scores and risk levels.
  • Reporting Layer: Generates an HTML risk report (risk_report.html).

The codebase is structured into src/ (logic), data/system_logs/ (sample logs) and diagrams/ (architecture assets), with a top-level README.md for documentation.

4. Detection and Risk Model

4.1 Anomaly Detection

The anomaly detector loads log entries and flags events using simple but enterprise-aligned rules:

  • High-risk event types such as privilege_escalation, data_export, suspicious_login.
  • Repeated failed login attempts indicating brute-force behaviour.

4.2 KRI-Based Risk Scoring

In the RiskScorer class, events are scored from 1 to 10 based on type and status. This reflects enterprise KRIs provided by a Senior Technology Risk Analyst:

  • KRI1: Repeated failed logins (brute force indicator).
  • KRI2: Privilege escalation events.
  • KRI3: Data export from sensitive systems.
  • KRI4: Suspicious login patterns (unusual IP or location).

Scores map to risk levels:

  • 1–3: Low
  • 4–6: Medium
  • 7–8: High
  • 9–10: Critical

5. Reporting and Outputs

The ReportGenerator class generates an HTML table summarising all anomalies, including:

  • Timestamp
  • User
  • Event type
  • Source IP
  • Status
  • Risk score and level
  • Reason for flagging (linked to KRIs)
