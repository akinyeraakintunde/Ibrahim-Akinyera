Flagship Project

Enterprise Risk Intelligence Engine

View on GitHub

An AI-powered technology risk and threat detection engine that ingests enterprise system logs, detects anomalies using expert-defined KRIs, scores events, and generates structured risk intelligence reports. Developed collaboratively with a Senior Technology Risk Analyst at CME Group.

Overview

What the system delivers

The engine ingests authentication, privilege, network and application events; applies rule-based and KRI-driven detection; and assigns risk scores on a scale from 1 to 10. Events are classified as Low, Medium, High or Critical and reported in a structured HTML risk report suitable for Technology Risk and Security teams.

Built with Python using a modular architecture: log ingestion, anomaly detection, risk scoring and reporting.

Role and responsibilities

My leadership in the project

I designed the architecture, implemented the detection engine, built the KRI-based scoring logic and the HTML reporting layer, and integrated expert-driven thresholds and KRIs into the system. I also led documentation and verification through GitHub, ensuring the project is auditable and externally verifiable.

  • End-to-end technical ownership of the engine.
  • Collaboration with a Senior Technology Risk Analyst.
  • Preparation of KPMG-standard written evidence and diagrams.
Architecture diagram

The engine is structured into four main layers: log ingestion, anomaly detection, risk scoring and reporting. This separation supports scalability, testing, and future ML model upgrades such as clustering or Isolation Forest.
